Choose one primary style family, then define any secondary influence narrowly.

- **Minimal / Swiss / flat**
  Best for enterprise, documentation, developer tools, finance, public services, and dense SaaS.
- **Trust and authority**
  Best for B2B services, legal, insurance, banking, healthcare, consulting, and government.
- **Soft UI / calm tactile**
  Best for wellness, mental health, beauty, hospitality, education, and premium service bookings.
- **Data-dense / monitoring**
  Best for analytics, BI, operations, finance dashboards, logistics, IoT, and admin panels.
- **Bento / modular showcase**
  Best for SaaS feature pages, product launches, portfolios, and mixed-content dashboards.
- **Motion-led / interactive demo**
  Best for creative tools, product demos, AI tools, media products, and highly visual launches.
- **Editorial / magazine**
  Best for portfolios, campaigns, hospitality, cultural products, newsletters, and narrative brands.
- **Immersive / 3D / configurator**
  Best for games, product visualization, automotive, furniture, fashion, spatial products, and premium commerce.
- **Raw / brutal / maximal**
  Best for youth brands, music, gaming, fashion drops, artist portfolios, and intentionally opinionated campaigns.
- **AI-native**
  Best for chat, copilots, generative tools, agent workflows, and automation platforms when the product actually uses AI.

Avoid trend bias:

- Do not use AI purple gradients unless the product has a reason to feel AI-native.
- Do not use glass, blur, or dark mode as decoration in high-trust products.
- Do not use playful tactile styles for regulated, money-sensitive, or safety-critical workflows.
- Do not use cinematic or scroll-heavy styles for task-heavy operational software.
