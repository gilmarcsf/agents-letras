Use the narrowest pattern that matches the core job.

- **Hero-centric landing**: one dominant visual promise, then proof and CTA. Best for branded launches and consumer landing pages.
- **Trust-and-proof landing**: promise plus credibility blocks, process, FAQ, and clear conversion. Best for B2B, consulting, healthcare, finance.
- **Feature showcase**: sequential modules for product capabilities, interaction demos, and proof. Best for SaaS and tools.
- **Interactive product demo**: prompt, mockup, video, sandbox, or generated result above supporting copy. Best for AI tools, developer tools, and product-led SaaS.
- **Search or marketplace landing**: search field, categories, featured listings, trust/safety, and seller/provider CTA. Best for directories, marketplaces, booking, real estate, and local services.
- **Pricing or comparison flow**: plans, comparison table, FAQ, objection handling, and clear recommendation. Best for subscriptions, B2B SaaS, and high-consideration purchases.
- **Operational workspace**: navigation plus primary working surface, filters, states, secondary inspector. Best for dashboards and internal tools.
- **Split detail workflow**: list/detail or canvas/inspector. Best for editors, asset managers, design tools.
- **Data monitoring workspace**: status overview, alert lane, real-time charts, drill-downs, and incident/context panels. Best for operations, IoT, logistics, finance, and DevOps.
- **Guided creation flow**: input, generation/build step, preview, edit controls, and export/share. Best for AI generation, content creation, and builder products.
- **Editorial sequence**: image, narrative, pull-quote, supporting modules, CTA. Best for campaigns, storytelling, hospitality, cultural products.
- **Immersive product experience**: full-bleed media, 3D configurator, guided tour, progress affordance, and skip path. Best for premium commerce, games, automotive, spatial, and highly visual launches.

Pre-delivery checklist:

- Primary action is obvious and singular on each screen.
- Clickable/tappable targets have visible states and at least 44px touch area when touch is relevant.
- Forms use visible labels, local errors, loading states, success/error feedback, and recovery paths.
- Responsive layouts work at 375px, 768px, 1024px, and 1440px without horizontal scroll.
- Text contrast meets WCAG AA; information is not conveyed by color alone.
- Motion uses transform/opacity where possible and respects reduced motion.
- Images, video, charts, and async content reserve layout space.
- Data views include loading, empty, error, filtered-empty, and permission states.
